19 · DISCOVERY CALL

30 minutes. Yes, no, or next.

The discovery call is not a pitch. It is a diagnostic. The goal is to know within 12 minutes whether AKHARA is the right fit. If yes, close. If no, refer them somewhere better.

00
PRE-CALL · 10 MIN BEFORE

Open buyer intelligence for their category. Have Loom demo built using their actual business in a tab. Slack on DND.

01
MIN 0-2 · THE FRAME

Set the rules of the call

"Thanks for jumping on. Quick frame for the next 30 minutes — my goal is to understand your business well enough to know if AKHARA is the right fit. If it is, I'll show you exactly what we'd build and what it costs. If it's not, I'll tell you and point you somewhere better. Sound good?"

Wait for confirmation. Don't move on without it.

02
MIN 2-12 · DIAGNOSE

Three questions only. Listen.

Q1: "What's the bottleneck in [their function] right now? Not the symptom — the actual function that's broken." Q2: "What have you tried already to fix it? Tools, hires, agencies — the full list." Q3: "If we fast-forward 6 months and this is solved — what does that actually look like? Specifically."
03
MIN 12-15 · REFRAME

Get the explicit "yeah, that's it"

"So if I heard you right — the real problem isn't [surface thing]. It's [actual thing]. And success looks like [their words back]. Right?"
04
MIN 15-23 · DEMO

60-sec Loom built using their actual business

"Let me show you exactly what this would look like for [their business name]." [Play Loom. Don't narrate over it. Let it land.] "Any questions on what you just saw?"
05
MIN 23-25 · PRICE

Whoever speaks next loses

"Based on what you've shared, this is The Operator. $5,000/month. 50% setup fee — that's $2,500 — paid before we start. Live in 14 days. 3-month minimum." [Say nothing. Wait.]
06
MIN 25-28 · CLOSE

Scale of 1 to 10

"On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you this solves the problem we just talked about?" → If 8+: "What's stopping us from starting Monday?" → If 6-7: "What would need to be true for it to be a 9?" → If 5 or below: "Sounds like we're not aligned yet. Walk me through what's missing."
07
MIN 28-30 · FOLLOW-UP

Never end with "let me know what you think"

YES: "Contract in your inbox within 4 hours. Setup fee invoice attached. Kickoff Monday. Sound good?" MAYBE: "Let's book a follow-up before this call ends. [Day] at [time]. Proposal in your inbox 24 hours before." NO: "Totally fair. What would change in 6 months that would make you revisit?"
SCENARIOS
20 · DIFFICULT SCENARIOS

Things will go sideways. Have the move ready.

AI sent something wrong to a client.
Pull log. Reply to client within 1 hour with the specific fix. Add the failure to the test matrix. Update system prompt. Run 5 similar test cases overnight. Never blame the AI in front of the client — own it.
Client requests scope creep without paying.
"Happy to add that. It's a [next tier] feature. Want me to spec it as a proper upgrade?" Never quietly absorb scope — it sets a precedent and trains them to ask for more.
Client wants a refund.
First find out why. 60% resolve before refund actually happens. If refund proceeds: refund unused portion only. Keep setup fee and any completed months. Document the feedback verbatim — it improves the product.
Client tries to cancel mid-contract.
Offer 3 options: (1) Pause — freeze the contract for up to 60 days, resume later. (2) Scope adjust — drop a channel or function, keep the same monthly. (3) Honour remaining weeks at full delivery, then a clean exit. Never let them ghost out — make it a decision.
Hot prospect ghosted after a great call.
Day 3: "Status?" — one word, one message. Day 7: "Closing the file Friday — yes, no, or ask me in [month]?" Then walk. Do not chase past Day 7. Half of these close in the 48 hours after a clean walk-away.
"Can you build this other thing for us?" — non-AI request.
If it's an AI Employee, scope it as a tier upgrade. If it's website / app / non-AI work: "Not our lane. I know 2 people who do that well — want intros?" Never dilute the offer to chase one-off revenue.

  2. Hard numbers — never "significantly", "many", "a lot"
  3. Short sentences — average 8-12 words
  4. Periods — kill commas, conjunctions, hedges
  5. AKHARA always ALL CAPS
  6. Active voice only — no "was built", "is being delivered"
  7. One idea per line — break paragraphs aggressively

Headline Formulas

  • Outcome + Timeframe: "$50K MRR. 90 days. No hires."
  • Replacement claim: "Your marketing team is overpriced. AKHARA replaces it."
  • Single-noun verdict: "Done. We ship in 14 days, not 14 weeks."

CTA Formulas

Verb + outcome + friction-killer.

  • "Book the demo. 60 seconds."
  • "See the system. No call required."
  • "Get the proposal. Same day."

Tense rules

Present and past only. Avoid future tense — AKHARA describes what IS, not what WILL BE. "We add $X MRR" beats "we will add $X MRR" every time.

Pronoun rules

"AKHARA" or "we" sparingly. Never "I" in a flexing way. Occasional "you" when speaking directly to the buyer. Never "us" — sounds like a startup.
